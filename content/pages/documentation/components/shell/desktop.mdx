## Guidelines

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

### ControlPanel Position

Choose the ControlPanel position based on navigation complexity. Top works for simple apps; Left suits applications with many navigation items or deep structures.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/RcpbbqfJwjNRlOwPhAX1im/Doku--Shell?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use the Top position for applications with few top-level navigation entries."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="caution"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/RcpbbqfJwjNRlOwPhAX1im/Doku--Shell?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="The Left position with only 2–3 navigation items takes up space without adding structural benefit. Consider Top instead."
	/>
</div>

### SubNavigation Placement

Choose a SubNavigation position that fits your information architecture and keep it consistent across all pages of your application.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/RcpbbqfJwjNRlOwPhAX1im/Doku--Shell?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Use SubNavigation Left for pages with many sub-level entries that benefit from a persistent vertical overview."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/RcpbbqfJwjNRlOwPhAX1im/Doku--Shell?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Do not switch SubNavigation position when navigating between top-level sections. Users expect the navigation layout to stay stable."
	/>
</div>

</CollapsibleWrapper>

### Navigation Depth

The Shell Desktop supports combining ControlPanel Navigation and SubNavigation in various ways. Both can have multiple levels independently. You can mirror the same structure in both, show only a subset, or distribute pages across the two navigation areas.
