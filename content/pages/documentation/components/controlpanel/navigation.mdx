## Guidelines

Choose the variant that fits your navigation structure:

- **Popover:** Flat to medium structures; reach deeper levels with a single click
- **Tree:** Complex hierarchies (3+ levels) where the full structure stays visible
- **DrillDown:** Many items with shallow depth (1–2 levels), shown one level at a time
- **FlatIcon:** A few equally weighted top-level destinations (2–6), accessed via icons

### Popover Horizontal Overflow

The horizontal Popover navigation in Desktop Top has limited space. Too many items require scroll chevrons which reduce discoverability of hidden items.

### Tree Item Overload

Tree navigation is designed for complex hierarchical structures. When the navigation has many items but a flat or shallow structure, DrillDown is more effective because it shows one level at a time and avoids overwhelming the user with a long expanded list.

### DrillDown Depth Limit

DrillDown shows one level at a time with a back-button pattern. With 4 or more levels, users lose orientation because they cannot see the full hierarchy. Tree keeps all levels visible and is better suited for deeply nested structures.

### FlatIcon Capacity

Keep the number of FlatIcon items manageable. On Mobile, the slot is technically limited (2–4 with text, 2–6 without text). On Desktop there is no hard limit, but too many items reduce clarity.
