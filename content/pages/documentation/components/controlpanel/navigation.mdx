- Popover: Flat-to-medium navigation with flyout submenus. Available in Desktop Horizontal, Desktop Vertical, and Mobile (Drawer with level-based back-button navigation)
- Tree: Deep hierarchical navigation with expand/collapse behavior. Available in Desktop Left only. Supports Single (accordion) or Multiple expand behavior
- FlatIcon: Icon-based tab navigation for 2–5 top-level destinations. Available in Desktop Left (vertical) and Mobile (horizontal bottom bar)

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

### Variant Selection

Choose the navigation variant based on your information architecture depth and the ControlPanel position. Popover works for flat-to-medium structures, Tree for deep hierarchies, FlatIcon for quick icon-based access.

		description="Use Popover for navigation with fewer items and shallow hierarchy (up to 2 levels) and Tree for structures with 3+ levels of nesting."

	/>
</div>

### Multi-Level Rule

Multi-level navigation can exist in either the ControlPanel or the SubNavigation. Applying multi-level in both creates conflicting navigation patterns and disorients users.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/pElrqVUyojrzYzSagyJPS6/Doku--ControlPanel?node-id=TODO-do&embed-host=share"
		description="Use multi-level navigation (Popover or Tree) in the ControlPanel only when the SubNavigation has a single-level or flat structure."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/pElrqVUyojrzYzSagyJPS6/Doku--ControlPanel?node-id=TODO-dont&embed-host=share"
		description="Do not use multi-level navigation in both the ControlPanel and the SubNavigation simultaneously."
	/>
</div>

### Tree Expand Behavior

Tree navigation supports Single (accordion-style, one group open at a time) or Multiple (several groups open simultaneously) expand behavior. Choose based on the number of groups and screen space.

		description="Use Single expand when the navigation has many groups and vertical space is limited — keeps the tree compact and focused."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/pElrqVUyojrzYzSagyJPS6/Doku--ControlPanel?node-id=TODO-do2&embed-host=share"

	/>
</div>

### FlatIcon Capacity

Keep the number of FlatIcon items manageable. On Mobile, the slot is technically limited (2–4 with text, 2–6 without text). On Desktop there is no hard limit, but too many items reduce clarity.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample

		description="Use a focused set of equally weighted top-level destinations in FlatIcon navigation."

	/>
</div>

### Popover Horizontal Overflow

The horizontal Popover navigation in Desktop Top has limited space. Too many items require scroll chevrons which reduce discoverability of hidden items.
