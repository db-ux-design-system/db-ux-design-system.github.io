Card groups related content into a contained surface to improve scanning and create clear visual structure. It can be static for content grouping or interactive as an entry point to deeper content, but should stay simple and consistent.

- Group related information and actions into a single, scannable block
- Present multiple items in a grid/list of consistent surfaces (card groups)
- Provide an interactive "preview" that links to details (interactive card)

## Usage Hints

###### Grouping

Use card when content belongs together and benefits from a distinct surface. Avoid wrapping everything in cards; too many cards reduce hierarchy and readability.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use cards to group a small set of related actions."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="Avoid placing every single UI element in a card, creating unnecessary visual noise."
	/>
</div>

###### Static or Interactive

Static cards are for grouping; interactive cards are for navigation or selection. If a card is interactive, the whole pattern must behave consistently and feel clickable.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Make interactive cards clearly clickable and apply one consistent interaction pattern per list/grid."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't mix clickable and non-clickable cards in the same group without clear differentiation."
	/>
</div>

###### Content Alignment

In card groups, align content consistently (headlines, media, actions) to support scanning. Maintain equal heights only when it improves comparability; otherwise let content size naturally.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1113&embed-host=share"
		description="Align titles and primary actions consistently across cards in the same grid."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1114&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't let each card use a different internal layout, making the grid hard to scan."
	/>
</div>

###### Card vs. List

Use lists for dense, comparable rows and quick scanning. Use cards when content needs grouping, hierarchy, or richer presentation (media, metadata, actions).

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1116&embed-host=share"
		description="Use a list for many similar items; use cards for richer previews and mixed content."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1117&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use cards for very dense datasets where a list/table would be more efficient."
	/>
</div>

###### Accessibility

If the card is interactive, it must be keyboard reachable and expose correct semantics (link or button). Focus must be visible, and nested interactive elements must not cause conflicting click targets.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1118&embed-host=share"
		description="Ensure one clear interactive target (or clearly separated targets) with visible focus and correct roles."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/2mqTMWDvKZPz91u1XrztAA/Doku--Card?node-id=3-1119&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't make the entire card clickable while also placing multiple nested links/buttons that compete for clicks."
	/>
</div>
