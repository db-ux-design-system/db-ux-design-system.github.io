The badge component is used to display short, contextual information such as status, category, or metadata in close relation to another element.

- Indicate a status (e.g., new), a count (e.g., 5), or a notification (e.g., unread) of an item
- Label or categorize content with a short descriptor
- Highlight supplementary information next to UI elements

## Usage Hints

###### Content

Badges should provide additional context and must not carry essential information on their own.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use a badge to display a status next to a list item or title."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use a badge as the only indicator for critical information."
	/>
</div>

###### Labels

Badge labels must be concise and scannable to ensure quick recognition.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Use one or two short words such as 'New' or 'Draft'."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Avoid long sentences or detailed explanations inside a badge."
	/>
</div>

###### Positioning

Place badges close to the element they describe, without breaking layout or alignment.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1113&embed-host=share"
		description="Align the badge consistently next to the related label or component."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1114&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't place badges far away or detached from their reference element."
	/>
</div>

###### Badge vs Tag

Use badges for status or metadata, and tags for filtering, grouping, or interactive categorization.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1116&embed-host=share"
		description="Use a badge to show status like 'Active' or 'Archived'."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1117&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use a badge when the element is meant to be clickable or removable."
	/>
</div>

###### Accessibility

Ensure badge information is available to assistive technologies and not conveyed by color alone.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample

	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/NRWHlLmwkLxnAqE5XSQlkV/Doku--Badge?node-id=3-1119&embed-host=share"

	/>
</div>
