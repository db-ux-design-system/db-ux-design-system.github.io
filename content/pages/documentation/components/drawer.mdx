## Guidelines

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

###### Usage

Use a drawer when users can continue their primary task and the drawer content is helpful but not essential. If the content is critical, blocking, or requires full attention, use a Dialog or dedicated page.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use the drawer for contextual content that provides additional details without leaving the main page."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use the drawer for important or critical information that needs to remain in view at all times."
	/>
</div>

###### Content

Drawers work best for lightweight actions (a few controls). Avoid complex, multi-step forms or long processes that become hard to complete and review in a constrained panel.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Use short optional forms and clear actions."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't run multi-step processes, lengthy forms, or complex validation-heavy flows in a drawer."
	/>
</div>

###### Placement

Regardless of which side the drawer is displayed from, it always takes up the entire width and height of the page. This full-screen display allows the drawer to get the right amount of attention.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1113&embed-host=share"
		description="The display of the drawers always overlays the entire page width and height, including the header."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1114&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't place the drawer only above a specific page area"
	/>
</div>

###### Closing

Always provide a way to close the drawer, such as clicking outside the drawer area. Include a close button in the header for keyboard accessibility. For drawers without a backdrop, a close button is required, since clicking outside will not close the drawer.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1116&embed-host=share"
		description="Always provide an option to collapse/close the drawer."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/y9c3hlB0PMdDYj3xLUCRWH/Doku--Drawer?node-id=3-1117&embed-host=share"
		description="Avoid removing the closing button without a valid reason."
	/>
</div>
