Accordion groups content into collapsible sections to save space and let users reveal details progressively. It is best for optional information and structured content where users do not need to see everything at once.

- Present FAQs or help content where users open only what they need
- Hide optional details behind clear section headlines (progressive disclosure)
- Improve readability when a page contains many content sections

## Guidelines

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

###### Collapsable Content

Use accordion for optional, expandable content. If users must compare multiple sections or see everything at once, use a visible list, sections, or cards instead.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use accordion for FAQs or 'Details' content that users open on demand."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use accordion when users need to scan or compare all sections simultaneously."
	/>
</div>

###### Headline wording

Headlines must describe the content inside and remain short and scannable. Avoid generic labels and avoid repeating the same word across all items.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Use descriptive headlines and keep it scannable."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Avoid vague headlines like “Information 1/2/3” or “More details”."
	/>
</div>

###### Use of variants

Select one accordion variant for a page/area and apply it consistently. Mixing variants makes scanning harder and weakens hierarchy.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=3-1113&embed-host=share"
		description="Use one consistent visual style for all accordion items in the same container."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/LMNsFNqNZRD6Ey1D1paqCV/Doku--Accordion?node-id=3-1114&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't mix different accordion variants within the same list without a clear reason."
	/>
</div>

###### Nesting

Nested accordions quickly become hard to understand and navigate. If nesting is unavoidable, keep it shallow and ensure clear hierarchy and spacing.
