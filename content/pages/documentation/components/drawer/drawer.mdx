## Guidelines

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

### Usage

Use a drawer when users can continue their primary task and the drawer content is helpful but not essential. If the content is critical, blocking, or requires full attention, use a Dialog or dedicated page.

### Content

Drawers work best for lightweight actions (a few controls). Avoid complex, multi-step forms or long processes that become hard to complete and review in a constrained panel.

### Placement

Regardless of which side the drawer is displayed from, it always takes up the entire width and height of the page. This full-screen display allows the drawer to get the right amount of attention.

### Size

Choose the drawer size according to the amount and complexity of the content. Use Small for short, focused tasks or simple contextual information. Use Medium for use cases with forms, lists, or more detailed content. Use Custom only when the predefined sizes don't fit the content or layout requirements. Use Full when users need a large working area, complex interactions, or when the drawer temporarily replaces the main page context.

### Closing

Always provide a way to close the drawer, such as clicking outside the drawer area. Include a close button in the header for keyboard accessibility. For drawers without a backdrop, a close button is required, since clicking outside will not close the drawer.
