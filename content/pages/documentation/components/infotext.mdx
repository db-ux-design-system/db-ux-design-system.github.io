Infotext provides a clear, compact way to communicate short supporting information or guidance directly in the interface. It helps users understand context, avoid errors, or interpret states without interrupting the flow.

- Give short hints or explanations near UI elements (e.g., what a setting changes)
- Provide brief warnings or important notes in context (e.g., constraints or limitations)
- Support form usage with concise guidance or validation-related context

## Guidelines

<CollapsibleWrapper collapsedHeight="320px">

###### Message

Keep infotext short and focused on one message. The text must communicate complete meaning on its own, even without the icon.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=1-1132&embed-host=share"
		description="Use one short sentence that clarifies meaning or next steps where the user needs it."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=1-1133&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use long paragraphs or multiple topics that require scrolling or heavy reading."
	/>
</div>

###### Emphasis

Use bold sparingly to highlight only a few key words. The message should remain understandable without emphasis and should not rely on styling for meaning.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1110&embed-host=share"
		description="Bold a single term or constraint that users must notice (e.g., 'Only once per day')."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1112&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't bold large parts of the sentence or use emphasis as the primary way to convey meaning."
	/>
</div>

###### Placement

Place infotext exactly where the decision is made. It must relate directly to the nearby UI element and not be used as general page messaging.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1113&embed-host=share"
		description="Attach infotext to the relevant section/field so users see it at the moment of need."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1114&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use infotext far away from the element it describes or as a generic announcement area."
	/>
</div>

###### Semantics

Choose the correct semantic meaning (e.g., neutral, info, warning, critical) so users can interpret urgency and intent. Semantics must be consistent across the product.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext-%E2%9C%85?node-id=3-1116&embed-host=share"
		description="Use warning/critical only when there is real risk or required attention, and explain what to do."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext-%E2%9C%85?node-id=3-1117&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use warning/critical styling for neutral tips or to add visual emphasis without real urgency."
	/>
</div>

###### Form fields

In forms, infotext can support understanding (format, constraints) and complement validation, but it must not replace labels or be used as placeholder-only guidance.

<div className="guideline-examples">
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="do"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1118&embed-host=share"
		description="Use infotext to clarify constraints near a field and keep validation errors in the validation pattern."
	/>
	<GuidelineExample
		variant="dont"
		figmaUrl="https://embed.figma.com/design/Iv8YHqQm11phTjrl656Sfx/Doku--Infotext?node-id=3-1119&embed-host=share"
		description="Don't use infotext as a substitute for the label or put essential instructions only into the placeholder."
	/>
</div>
