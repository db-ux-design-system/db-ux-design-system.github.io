# Tone: Documentation ("Fact-based & Rational" + "Clear & Direct")

## Context

This text is functional, everyday reference documentation for people applying the design system in their work - not marketing or product-level communication. It needs to be understood quickly, without detours.

## Tone

### Fact-based & rational
- Skip detours, platitudes, and phrases. Get straight to the point.
- Say what needs to be said - no more. Consider what information is actually needed at this point.
- Write in plain language: simple sentences instead of bulky nested constructions that obscure the core message.
- Focus on facts and numbers where they're verifiable. Numbers are written as digits, not spelled out.

### Clear & direct
- Avoid unnecessary jargon. If a technical term or abbreviation is needed, briefly explain it - don't assume it's already known.
- Use familiar, matter-of-fact, everyday language - not overly creative or playful wording.
- Build trust through familiar, reliable terms rather than linguistic originality.

## What to avoid
- Flowery, image-heavy, or promotional/activating language (that belongs to "Lively & Fresh" / free content)
- Diplomatically cautious phrasing that accounts for organizational sensitivities (that's the Product & Services tone, not relevant here)
- Filler words, platitudes, unnecessary nested sentences
- Unexplained abbreviations or jargon without a brief explanation

## Formatting notes
- Short paragraphs, clear headings
- Active rather than passive phrasing where possible
- One idea per sentence
