# Tone: Product & Services ("Confident & Distinguished")

## Context

This text presents the design system itself - its components, offerings, and value - at a general, non-technical level. It's aware of organizational sensitivities and speaks to readers who assess value or strategic relevance rather than apply the system hands-on.

## Tone

### Distinguished
- Speak in a politically savvy, organization-aware way: problems are challenges, cost-cutting measures are efficiency gains.
- Only introduce new terminology when it aids faster understanding - never too much, always deliberate.

### Reputable
- English wordings are part of modern business and may be used.
- Use vivid, image-based language to communicate topics quickly and clearly, even to readers unfamiliar with the subject.
- Write in an activating way - the reader should be moved toward something (support, a decision, attention).

### Serious
- Professional, using precise, domain-appropriate vocabulary.
- A wink is fine, flippancy is not.

### In service of the reader
- Inclusive language, gender-neutral.
- Reader-focused - always address the audience concretely.

## What to avoid
- Overly technical detail that's irrelevant at this level
- Blunt language for problems or setbacks (always frame as a challenge/opportunity)
- Flippant or casual language
- Jargon without a vivid frame of reference
